# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Chandra serves teachers and students in classroom settings. Teachers use it to set up class-specific AI tutors, upload course materials, define tutoring behavior, manage access, and review student conversations for learning signals. Students use it as a guided chat workspace for class questions, assignments, and source-grounded help, with the expectation that the tutor supports their thinking instead of replacing it.

## Product Purpose

Chandra helps teachers configure source-grounded AI tutors that guide students through learning without simply giving answers. The platform gives teachers control over answer policy, source usage, tutor behavior, class materials, model settings, and student access, while surfacing confusion, misuse, and support needs through conversation review, learning profiles, and class-level summaries.

Success means students receive useful, rigorous help that keeps them engaged in the work, and teachers can understand where learners are struggling without turning the product into punitive monitoring.

## Brand Personality

Calm, rigorous, teacher-trusted.

The product should feel composed and credible under classroom pressure. It should communicate expert confidence without sounding clinical, corporate, or patronizing. The tone should respect teachers' professional judgment and students' agency.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI SaaS styling, over-polished productivity theater, childish edtech visuals, gamified classroom gimmicks, and interfaces that imply surveillance or punishment. Avoid making AI feel magical, autonomous, or more central than the teacher's instructional intent.

## Design Principles

1. Teacher intent is the control surface: settings, reviews, and tutor behavior should make instructional choices explicit and reversible.
2. Keep students doing the thinking: chat responses, feedback, and UI states should guide, scaffold, and check understanding before revealing conclusions.
3. Make evidence easy to inspect: teacher-facing summaries should trace back to conversations, source usage, student signals, and class materials.
4. Earn trust through restraint: prioritize clarity, predictable controls, and calm density over decorative novelty.
5. Support intervention without surveillance: show what teachers need to help students while avoiding punitive framing or unnecessary exposure.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for product surfaces. Support reduced motion, keyboard navigation, visible focus states, sufficient contrast across light and dark appearances, and color-blind-safe status cues that do not depend on color alone. Student and teacher workflows should remain usable on smaller screens, with clear labels and error messages for authentication, class access, chat limits, uploads, and settings changes.
